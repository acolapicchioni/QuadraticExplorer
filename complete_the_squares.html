Your Easy Guide to Solving Quadratic Equations (ax2 + bx + c = 0)

Hey Peter! Got a tricky quadratic equation? No problem! Let's learn how to solve it using a cool method called "Completing the Square." Follow these simple steps:

Step 1: Get Ready! Set Up Your Equation ‚öôÔ∏è

The goal here is to get your equation into the form: x2 + bx = c.

  1. Isolate x2 and x terms: If your equation is ax2 + bx + c = 0, move the constant term (c) to the right side:
    ax2 + bx = -c
  2. Leading Coefficient Check: If there's a number in front of x2 (the 'a' value) and it's not 1, divide every single term in the equation by 'a'.
    ax2a + bxa = -ca
    This simplifies to: x2 + (ba)x = -ca
Example: Let's solve 2x2 + 12x - 14 = 0
  1. Move constant: 2x2 + 12x = 14
  2. 'a' is 2, so divide everything by 2:
    2x22 + 12x2 = 142
    Which gives us: x2 + 6x = 7
    (Now our equation is in the x2 + bx = c form, where b=6 and c=7)

Step 2: Find the Magic Ingredient! ‚ú®

This special number will help us "complete the square."

  1. Identify 'b': Look at the coefficient of your x term (the number next to x). In x2 + bx = c, this is 'b'.
  2. Halve it: Calculate (b2).
  3. Square it: Calculate (b2)2. This is your magic ingredient!
Example (Continuing with x2 + 6x = 7):
  1. Our 'b' is 6.
  2. Halve it: (62) = 3.
  3. Square it: (3)2 = 9.

Our magic ingredient is 9!

Step 3: Add the Magic! ü™Ñ‚ûï

Add the magic ingredient you just found to both sides of your equation. This keeps the equation balanced!

x2 + bx + (b2)2 = c + (b2)2

Example (Continuing):

Add 9 to both sides of x2 + 6x = 7:
x2 + 6x + 9 = 7 + 9
x2 + 6x + 9 = 16

Step 4: Factor Power! ü¶∏‚Äç‚ôÇÔ∏è

The left side of your equation is now a "perfect square trinomial." This means it can be factored neatly.

It will always factor into the form: (x + b2)2
(Notice that b2 is the number you got in Step 2 before squaring it!)

Example (Continuing):

Factor x2 + 6x + 9:
Since our b2 was 3, this factors to (x + 3)2.
So, our equation becomes: (x + 3)2 = 16

Step 5: Almost There! Solve for x üèÅ

Now, we isolate x.

  1. Square Root Both Sides: Take the square root of both sides of the equation. Don't forget the ± (plus or minus) on the right side, because both positive and negative numbers give a positive result when squared!
    √(x + b2)2 = ±√(right side)
    x + b2 = ±√(right side)
  2. Isolate x: Subtract the b2 term from both sides to get x by itself.
    x = -b2 ±√(right side)
Example (Continuing):

Equation: (x + 3)2 = 16

  1. Square root both sides:
    √(x + 3)2 = ±√16
    x + 3 = ±4
  2. Isolate x (subtract 3 from both sides):
    x = -3 ±4

Step 6: The Solutions! Your Answers Revealed üèÜ

You're at the final step! Calculate the two possible values for x using the ±.

  1. Solution 1: Use the '+' sign.
  2. Solution 2: Use the '-' sign.
Example (Continuing with x = -3 ±4):
  1. Solution 1 (with +):
    x1 = -3 + 4
    x1 = 1
  2. Solution 2 (with -):
    x2 = -3 - 4
    x2 = -7

So, the solutions to 2x2 + 12x - 14 = 0 are x = 1 and x = -7. You did it! üéâ

Quick Recap! üìù

  1. Form x2 + bx = c: Adjust the equation.
  2. Magic Ingredient: Find and add (b2)2 to both sides.
  3. Factor: Rewrite the left side as (x + b2)2.
  4. Square Root: Take ±√ of both sides.
  5. Isolate x: Get x alone.
  6. Two Solutions: Calculate your two answers!
