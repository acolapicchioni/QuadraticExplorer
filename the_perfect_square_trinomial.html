What's a Perfect Square Trinomial?

A perfect square trinomial is a trinomial (an expression with three terms) that results from squaring a binomial (an expression with two terms).

It has two main forms:

  • (a + b)2 = a2 + 2ab + b2
  • (a - b)2 = a2 - 2ab + b2

Key things to look for:

  • The first term (a2) is a perfect square.
  • The last term (b2) is a perfect square.
  • The middle term (2ab or -2ab) is twice the product of the square roots of the first and last terms.
